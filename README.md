I notice that most of the games I'm interested in are multiplayer, very colorful and expressive, have various character customization options, and smooth, easy to understand movement and interactions. They also all have a storytelling element that drives a deeper lore behind each design of food or environment. Gameplay in most of the games are fluid and wins feels satisfying through music and awards/points. They are also competitive or have some intellectual difficulty in order to complete successfully. Each person's experience playing these games are also different as they have flexibility to forge their own path. 
2. We are interested in creating a game that is smooth and easy to understand in regards to player movement and interactions. We also want it to be challenging to some degree, where varying effort results in varying results. Aesthetically, we also desire the game to look clean and polished, with consistent visuals.
3. The LA's tastes in games are similar to mine as they also like Nintendo games and multiplayer games. They also agree that challenge in games is important to drive players to continue playing to feel a sense of achievement.
